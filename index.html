About Airbus A320 PaperCraft

The Airbus A320 is one of the most popular aircraft in the world. Now, you can replicate it in paper form! Our model takes inspiration from the real Airbus A320, ensuring every fold helps bring the aircraft to life.

Real Airbus A320 vs. Papercraft Model

  • Wingspan: 35.8m (Real) | 25cm (Papercraft)
  • Length: 37.57m (Real) | 28cm (Papercraft)
  • Cruise Speed: 828 km/h (Real) | 0.5 m/s (Papercraft, depending on throw!)
  • Engine Type: Twin-engine jet (Real) | Paper folds (Papercraft)

How to Build the Airbus A320 PaperCraft

  1. Start with a clean A4 sheet of paper. Place it on a flat surface.
  2. Fold the paper in half lengthwise, then unfold to leave a crease in the center.
  3. Fold the top two corners to meet the center crease, creating a triangle shape at the top.
  4. Fold the triangle down to create the nose of the aircraft.
  5. Continue folding the edges towards the center to refine the wings.
  6. Final adjustments: Fold the wings and make sure everything is symmetrical.

Primary Parts of an Aircraft and Their Usefulness

  • Fuselage: The main body of the aircraft, housing passengers, cargo, and cockpit.
  • Wings: Provide lift and are responsible for keeping the aircraft in the air.
  • Ailerons: Located on the wings, control roll and help turn the aircraft.
  • Elevators: Located on the tail, control the pitch of the aircraft (up and down movement).
  • Rudder: Located on the vertical tail fin, controls the yaw (left and right movement) of the aircraft.
  • Flaps: Extend from the rear of the wings to increase lift at lower speeds, useful during takeoff and landing.
  • Landing Gear: Supports the aircraft while on the ground and allows for safe takeoffs and landings.
  • Engines: Provide the necessary thrust to propel the aircraft forward.
  • Tailplane: Helps stabilize the aircraft and control its pitch.
